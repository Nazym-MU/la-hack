# Superposition (study notes)

A qubit isn't just 0 or 1 — it's a linear combination of both until measured.
State written as α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, with |α|² + |β|² = 1. Measurement collapses it to
one outcome with those probabilities. The Bloch sphere is the mental picture:
any pure state is a point on the surface of a sphere.

Keep confusing myself: superposition is NOT "the qubit is secretly one value we
don't know" — the interference effects only make sense if both amplitudes are
really there at once.
